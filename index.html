Nenet

Born Maria Florencia Barrios in Buenos Aires, June 2, 1986, Nenet is an argentine writer, singer and performer. Her first steps in poetry were tearjerking odes to denim jackets, intangible idols and highschool. She edited fanzines of her own poetry, pop culture and surrealist musings. In 2005 she started "Vida Yummy", an online archive that narrated the misfortunes of work, love and reviewed Buenos Aires underground musical scene. After a period studying Literature in the University of Buenos Aires (UBA), she edited an EP together with the band Amenaza Menor. With the writer and visual artist Liv Schulman she published the magazine "Pobre, Feo & Elegante". In 2012, after moving to the US, she published the bilingual feminist fanzine "Leda", followed by "Medea". In 2014 she taught "Poetry & Sodomy: Argentine Writers of the 20th Century" for Knowledge Commons DC, followed by "Pessimism & Pie: Pessimism as a Philosophy of Self Conduct" in 2016. Later that year, she started the rock n' roll band Park Snakes, along with guitarist Jason Coile and drummer Van Hillard. The band edited their self-titled EP via Blight Records and is currently producing what will be their first LP. Nenet currently resides in Washington, DC.
